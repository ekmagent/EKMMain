## 5. Ten Seeded Q&A Pairs

Post the questions from a personal Google account, answer from the business profile.
Space them out — 1–2 per week, not all at once.

**Q1. Is your help really free? How do you get paid?**
A: Yes — you never pay us anything. Carriers pay brokers a commission when you enroll, and the rate is essentially the same across carriers for the same plan type, so we have no reason to steer you. Your premium is identical whether you use a broker or go to the carrier directly. We're a licensed insurance broker, not affiliated with or endorsed by Medicare or any government agency.

**Q2. What's the difference between Medigap Plan G and Plan N?**
A: Plan G covers everything Medicare-approved after you meet the annual Part B deductible. Plan N has a lower premium but adds copays (up to $20 for office visits, up to $50 for ER) and doesn't cover Part B excess charges. Which one wins depends on your premiums locally and how often you see doctors — we'll show you the actual math for your ZIP code.

**Q3. When is the best time to enroll in a Medicare Supplement plan?**
A: Your 6-month Medigap Open Enrollment Period, which starts the month you're 65+ and enrolled in Part B. During that window carriers must accept you regardless of health. Outside it, most applications go through medical underwriting (with some federal guaranteed-issue exceptions). If you're approaching Medicare, call before your Part B starts — applying early can lock in your rate.

**Q4. Do you work with one insurance company or many?**
A: Many. We're independent — appointed with multiple Medigap and Medicare Advantage carriers and licensed in 35 states. We do not offer every plan available in your area; you can always contact Medicare.gov, 1-800-MEDICARE, or your local SHIP for help with plan choices.

**Q5. Can I switch Medigap carriers if mine keeps raising rates?**
A: Often, yes. In most states switching means answering health questions (medical underwriting). If you're reasonably healthy, moving to a carrier with the same plan letter at a lower filed rate is common — the coverage is standardized, so Plan G is Plan G. We check the new carrier's rate-increase history too, so you're not trading one problem for another.

**Q6. Is Medicare Advantage or a Medicare Supplement better?**
A: Neither is "better" — they're different trade-offs. Advantage plans usually have low or $0 premiums but networks, prior authorizations, and out-of-pocket costs when you use care. Medigap costs more monthly but gives you nearly any doctor that takes Medicare with minimal bills. The right answer depends on your health, budget, doctors, and travel. We walk through both honestly.

**Q7. Do you help with prescription drug (Part D) plans?**
A: Yes. We enter your exact medication list and pharmacies and compare total annual cost — premium plus copays — across available Part D plans. The lowest-premium plan is frequently not the cheapest overall for your specific prescriptions.

**Q8. I live in New Jersey — is Medigap different here?**
A: In some helpful ways. NJ Medigap plans are community-rated, meaning your premium isn't based on your age. NJ also prohibits tobacco-based rate differences during your 6-month open enrollment window. The standard federal rules still apply — the 6-month Medigap Open Enrollment Period and federal guaranteed-issue rights — so timing still matters.

**Q9. What states are you licensed in?**
A: New Jersey and 34 other states — including Pennsylvania, Ohio, Texas, Florida, and most of the East and Midwest. You can verify any producer license at NIPR.com (NPN 20586791).

**Q10. What should I bring to a phone appointment?**
A: Three things: your doctor list, your prescription list (names and dosages), and your Medicare card or Part A/B start dates if you have them. With those we can compare plans accurately in one call. There's no cost and no obligation.
