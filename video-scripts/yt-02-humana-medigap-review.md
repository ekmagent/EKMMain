### CHAPTER 1: Who Actually Underwrites a Humana Medigap Policy (0:55)

First thing most people don't know. In Pennsylvania, Texas, and several other states, Humana Medicare Supplement policies are marketed as "Humana Achieve" — and they're legally underwritten by Humana Insurance Company of Kentucky, NAIC code 60219. The name on your policy paperwork may not match the brand name on the marketing materials. Same brand family, but it's worth knowing whose name is on your contract.

The company facts: AM Best rating of A — that's "Excellent" — with a Stable outlook. Standard & Poor's rating of A. The Medigap subsidiary was established in 2004. CSG's customer satisfaction ratio for Humana is 0.83.

[ON SCREEN: "AM Best: A (Stable) · S&P: A · CSG customer satisfaction ratio: 0.83"]

Products in the states I reviewed: Plan G, Plan N, and High-Deductible Plan G, with Plan A in some states. Household discount ranges from 7.1% in New Jersey to 12.1% in Pennsylvania, Ohio, and Texas.

And one structural fact that matters more than any single rate: Humana uses attained-age pricing in all four states I reviewed. That means your premium automatically rises every year because you got a year older — separate from, and on top of, any filed rate increase. Over a 15-to-20-year Medigap tenure, that compounds meaningfully against community-rated or issue-age carriers.

### CHAPTER 2: The One Concept You Need — Loss Ratios and the Adverse Selection Spiral (2:40)

Before the state numbers, sixty seconds on the mechanism, because once you see it, every rate increase in this video will make sense.

A loss ratio is claims paid divided by premiums collected. If a carrier's loss ratio in a state hits 100%, they're paying out every premium dollar in claims — they're losing money on that block of business. They can't absorb that forever, so they file a rate increase with the state insurance department. Then the healthy policyholders — the ones who can pass underwriting — shop around and leave for cheaper carriers. The pool that stays behind skews sicker. Loss ratio climbs again. Another increase. That's the adverse selection spiral, and it's the single most important dynamic to understand before you pick a Medigap carrier.

[ON SCREEN: "Loss ratio = claims paid ÷ premiums collected. Over 100% = losing money = rate increase coming"]

In the last 12 months, Humana filed Plan G rate increases of 16% to 25% in every single state I reviewed. Let's see why, state by state.
