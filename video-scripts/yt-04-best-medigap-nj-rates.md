### CHAPTER 1: The Diet Coke Rule — Plan Letters Are Standardized (1:00)

The single most important fact in Medigap shopping: a Plan G from Aetna covers the exact same benefits as a Plan G from Mutual of Omaha or anyone else. Federal law standardizes every plan letter. Think of it like a can of Diet Coke — same product, different price depending on where you buy it. Your job is not to find "better coverage." Your job is to find the best price from a stable carrier.

The plans available in New Jersey for 2026:

- Plan A — basic benefits only. For the very healthy with minimal use.
- Plan B — basic plus the Part A deductible.
- Plan G — everything except the Part B deductible. The most popular plan and the best value for most people.
- Plan N — Plan G minus small copays, for lower premiums.
- Plans K and L — partial cost-sharing plans with out-of-pocket maximums, for the budget-conscious and healthy.

### CHAPTER 4: Why the Gap Is So Wide — Three Pricing Methods in One State (7:15)

New Jersey allows Medigap carriers to use three different pricing methodologies in parallel, and that mix is the biggest driver of the spread:

- Community-rated carriers charge every enrollee the same base rate regardless of age. Older applicants benefit most; at 65, these carriers can actually look expensive next to age-based alternatives.
- Issue-age-rated carriers lock your rate at the age you enrolled. Enroll at 65, and your premium never gets an age-based bump — only general rate adjustments.
- Attained-age-rated carriers raise your premium every year as you age. They're often the cheapest at 65 — and the most expensive at 75.

[ON SCREEN: "Community · Issue-age · Attained-age — all three coexist in NJ"]

That last one is the trap: the cheapest Plan G today is not always the cheapest Plan G at age 75. Rate-increase history matters just as much — some carriers raise premiums 2 to 4% a year, others 10 to 15%, and over ten years that difference compounds dramatically. Same starting quote, wildly different destination. A broker can pull each carrier's rate-increase history from the CSG database before you commit — not just today's lowest number.

### CHAPTER 5: The Household Discount Most NJ Applicants Miss (9:15)

Here's free money. In my April 2026 snapshot, 4 of the licensed NJ Plan G carriers offer a household discount, ranging from 5% to 11.1% off the monthly premium at age 65.

The reason people miss it: eligibility rules are different at every carrier. Some require both spouses to apply. Some accept civil unions or cohabitation. Some only require another adult in your household to have — or be applying for — Medigap coverage with any carrier. If you apply direct on one insurer's website, nobody walks you through which carrier's rules your household actually fits. It's the second-largest lever after carrier selection itself.

### CHAPTER 6: New Jersey's Specific Rules and Protections (10:30)

Three things specific to New Jersey.

First — tobacco-neutral open enrollment. New Jersey is one of the few states that prohibits tobacco rate differentials during the Medigap Open Enrollment Period. Enroll during your 6-month OEP and tobacco and non-tobacco applicants pay the same rate for identical coverage.

Second — under-65 access. New Jersey requires carriers to offer Medigap to beneficiaries under 65 who are on Medicare due to disability, with access windows tied to Part B enrollment. That is not federally required — many states don't have it.

Third — and I need to be precise here, because this gets misstated all over the internet: New Jersey does not have year-round guaranteed issue. That's New York and Connecticut. In New Jersey, your protection is the standard federal 6-month Medigap Open Enrollment Period — the six months starting when you're both 65 and enrolled in Part B. During that window, no carrier can deny you or charge more based on health. Miss it, and outside of federal guaranteed-issue triggers, carriers can underwrite. That window is the most important timing decision in NJ Medigap shopping.
